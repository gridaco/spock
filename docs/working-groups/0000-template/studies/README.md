# WG NNNN studies

This directory contains reviewable research produced under the WG charter.
Studies inform the final report and a possible RFD; they are non-normative and
do not express a Language Design Committee decision.

Name a study for its question, for example `prior-art.md`,
`compatibility-cases.md`, or `prototype-results.md`. Give each study an author,
date, and review state.

## Study structure

Each study should contain:

1. **Question** — the bounded question being investigated.
2. **Method** — sources, fixtures, experiments, and selection criteria.
3. **Evidence** — observations with primary-source links or reproducible
   artifacts where possible.
4. **Findings** — what the evidence supports, separated from preference.
5. **Counterevidence and alternatives** — facts or cases that weaken the
   leading interpretation.
6. **Limitations** — what was not measured, represented, or verified.
7. **Implications** — which charter questions this informs without pretending
   to decide them.
8. **Follow-ups** — additional work, owner, and target date when applicable.

Experiments must record enough setup, input, and expected output to be repeated.
Examples should include boundaries and counterexamples, not only a favorable
happy path. Meeting notes may link a study, but discussion alone is not a
substitute for writing the evidence down here.
