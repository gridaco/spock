> an RFD, or authorize graduation into supported implementation.

## Executive summary

Summarize the problem, strongest findings, important uncertainty, and the WG's
recommendation without hiding dissent.

## Charter and work completed

Restate the chartered questions and scope. List studies, experiments, meeting
records, and other deliverables. Identify planned work that was not completed.

## Findings

For each material finding, link the supporting evidence and state its limits.
Separate observed facts, inferences, and preferences.

## Viable approaches

Describe each credible approach, including making no language change. Compare
their semantic fit, compatibility, implementation cost, risks, and long-term
consequences.

## Recommendation

Choose one:

- recommend that a sponsor advance a specific RFD;
- recommend no language change;
- recommend later study after named evidence or dependencies exist; or
- report no useful conclusion.

Explain why the evidence supports this recommendation. A recommendation does
not guarantee sponsorship or acceptance.

## Dissent and minority views

Preserve material disagreement in its strongest form and link supporting
evidence. Write “none recorded” only after participants have had an opportunity
to contribute.

## Unresolved questions and evidence gaps

- Question or gap, its consequence, and what could resolve it.

## Proposed RFD scope

If recommending an RFD, state the proposed decision boundary, goals, non-goals,
and which specification areas it could eventually affect. Do not assign an RFD
number here.
