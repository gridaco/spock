## Problem

State the concrete language problem and who encounters it. Explain why existing
Spock and ordinary individual research are insufficient.

## Questions to answer

1. A bounded research or design question.
2. A question whose answer would distinguish viable approaches.

## Scope

- Work this WG will perform.
- Language and implementation boundaries it may investigate.

## Non-goals

- Adjacent question this WG will not absorb.
- Language decision this WG has no authority to make.

## Deliverables

- Named study, dataset, prototype, or comparison.
- Meeting records for substantive sessions.
- `FINAL-REPORT.md` summarizing evidence, options, dissent, and recommendation.

## Evidence standard

State what would count as meaningful evidence: primary references, executable
fixtures, compatibility analysis, user cases, performance measurements, or
other reproducible work. State known limits and what would falsify a working
hypothesis.

## Membership and roles

List the sponsor, chair, recorder/editor, and members. Describe how additional
contributors may submit evidence. Membership supplies labor and perspective;
it grants no design authority or vote over an RFD.

## Operating cadence

State the expected meeting cadence, offline location or medium when relevant,
how agendas and pre-reading are shared, and how asynchronous review occurs.
Every substantive meeting must produce a repository note.

## Decision boundary

This WG may reach non-binding conclusions and recommend an RFD. It may not
accept or reject an RFD, modify `docs/spec/`, authorize graduation into
supported implementation, or make a decision on behalf of the Language Design
Committee.
